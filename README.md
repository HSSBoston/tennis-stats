## Event-Driven Gain in Expectancy (EDGE): A Probablistic Metric for Comprehensive Player Performance in Tennis

Traditional tennis statistics, such as aces, double faults, winners, and unforced errors, provide intuitive box-score summaries of player performance. However, these primitive metrics evaluate isolated aspects of performance rather than summarizing a player's overall performance. As a result, comparing individual players often requires tables or other visualizations that combine multiple separate metrics. This makes interpretation less direct.

Composite metrics address this limitation by combining match-level outcomes or point-level events (e.g., observed serve/return/point outcomes) into a single numerical measure. For example, metrics such as Dominance Ratio (DR) summarize performance more directly than primitive metrics. However, these metrics are deterministic: they are calculated from observed outcomes and do not fully account for the score context in which each point occurs. For example, a winner at 40–0 and a winner on break point may have very different effects on the game, even though both are recorded as one winner.
